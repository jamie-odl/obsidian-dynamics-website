WHAT IS OBSIDIAN

Two products. One company. Clear scope.

SkyGrid detects aircraft anomalies in the browser — loiter, ghost tracks, squawk codes, GPS jamming, and more. BLACKGLASS audits Linux SSH posture, freezes baselines, and surfaces configuration drift with severity before it becomes an incident.

Who

Operations teams, risk teams, and engineers who need faster decisions with less noise.

What

Aircraft anomaly intelligence and Linux fleet integrity — structured signals, not raw data dumps.

Why

Lead time matters. Both products are built around surfacing risk before it compounds.

PRODUCTS

Clearly defined products and scope.

SkyGrid

Browser-first aircraft anomaly monitoring. Eight detection classes covering loiter, squawk codes, ghost tracks, GPS jamming, ICAO spoofing, and more.

BLACKGLASS

Linux integrity monitoring — SSH posture, agentless baselines, drift detection with severity, and audit-ready evidence exports.
USE CASES

Problem, solution, outcome.

Missed anomalies

SkyGrid surfaces loiter, squawk, and ghost track events the moment they cross thresholds — before the situation is obvious to anyone watching manually.

Fragmented posture

BLACKGLASS gives SSH and configuration drift a single workflow: connect, baseline, detect, remediate, export evidence. No CMDB project required.

Integration overhead

Both products use the same API conventions — consistent schema, auth, and replay so teams wire it in once and extend it without rewrites.
