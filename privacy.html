2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide

  • Contact information: Name, email address, and message content when you submit our contact form.
  • Account information: Email address when you sign in via magic link to access the Intelligence API, admin workspace, or related allowlisted pages. We do not use passwords for these accounts.
  • Billing information: If you subscribe to paid access, payment details are processed by our third-party payment processor. We do not store full payment card numbers.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Usage and access logs: Pages visited, API requests (endpoint, timestamp, response status), features used, and interaction patterns. For authenticated access we log your email and role tier to enforce allowlists and investigate abuse.
  • Device and browser information: Browser type, operating system, and screen resolution where available from standard HTTP headers.
  • Network information: IP address, access times, referring URLs, and request parameters. We use this for security, rate limiting, and service reliability.
  • Authentication events: Magic-link requests, sign-in outcomes, and session activity are recorded in server logs for security auditing.

2.3 Cookies and similar technologies

We use the following on allowlisted and marketing pages:

  • od_dev_session — an essential, HttpOnly session cookie set after you verify a magic link. It keeps you signed in for up to 12 hours. It is required for authenticated API and workspace access.
  • od_analytics_consent — stored in localStorage, not a cookie. It records whether you accepted or declined optional analytics on marketing pages.

Where you allow optional analytics, we may use first-party page-view metrics via Vercel Web Analytics to improve the website. You can decline analytics using the cookie banner; browser controls can clear stored preferences at any time.

3. Public Council Data We Process

Separately from your personal data, we collect and process publicly published council documents — IFS spreadsheets, PDFs, and related landing pages — to build our developer contributions dataset. This material is already in the public domain.

  • We discover and fetch documents from council websites using automated crawl workers (including dedicated Raspberry Pi devices on infrastructure we operate).
  • We extract structured line items, normalise categories, and attach provenance (source URL, page or table references where available).
  • We may retain archived source files and optional screenshots to support evidence links exposed through the Intelligence API.
  • Structured records are stored in a PostgreSQL database on infrastructure we operate. The marketing site and API edge functions are hosted on Vercel; backend services may be reached via a secure Cloudflare Tunnel connection.

Council IFS data may occasionally mention project or site names but is not collected to profile individuals. If you believe personal data has been included in our public dataset in error, contact us and we will review promptly.

Methodology and coverage details are published on our Research & data page.

5. Legal Basis for Processing

Under UK GDPR, we rely on the following bases — described in plain terms:

  • Contract: We need your email and usage data to give you the access you signed up for (for example, API or admin workspace use under agreed terms).
  • Legitimate interests: We process logs and security data to keep the service running safely, prevent abuse, and improve reliability — provided this does not override your privacy rights.
  • Consent: Optional analytics on marketing pages, and any other processing where we ask for and you give specific consent.
  • Legal obligation: Where we must retain or disclose information to comply with UK law.

Processing of public council documents is based on our legitimate interest in organising published records for research and journalism, and on the public nature of the source material.

7. Data Retention

  • Account and allowlist data: Retained while your access is active, plus a reasonable period after removal for audit and dispute resolution.
  • Session cookies: Expire after 12 hours or when you sign out.
  • Server and API logs: Typically retained for up to 90 days unless needed longer for security investigation or legal compliance.
  • Contact form submissions: Retained for up to 24 months unless you request earlier deletion.
  • Public council records: Retained while they remain useful for the dataset and provenance, and updated or removed when superseded by newer published sources.

8. Data Security
