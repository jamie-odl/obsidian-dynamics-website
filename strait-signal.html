COVERAGE

Seven Critical Chokepoints

Real-time monitoring of the world's most strategically important maritime corridors.

Suez Canal

The world's busiest artificial waterway. ~15% of global trade transits daily. Queue depth and northbound/southbound convoy tracking.

Bab el-Mandeb

Gateway to the Red Sea and Suez. ~10% of global seaborne oil transits. Houthi-related disruption intelligence and reroute risk scoring.

Singapore Strait

The world's busiest shipping lane. Over 1,000 vessels transit daily. Congestion monitoring and anchorage queue depth tracking.

Strait of Malacca

Connects the Indian Ocean to the Pacific. ~25% of global trade. Draught limitations and seasonal weather risk impact scoring.

Panama Canal

Connects Atlantic and Pacific. Drought-driven transit restrictions and lock queue monitoring. Historical baseline against 2023-24 drought disruptions.

Strait of Hormuz

~21% of global oil consumption transits daily. Geopolitical tension monitoring and tanker traffic flow analysis.

Port of Rotterdam

Europe's largest port and key North Sea gateway. Berth availability, container dwell time, and feeder route impact tracking.

What Strait-Signal Delivers

Anchorage Queue Tracking

Delay Delta Baselines

Compares current transit times against rolling historical baselines. Positive delta means delays are building. Negative means the corridor is clearing.

METHODOLOGY

How Intelligence Is Validated

Every alert is grounded in observable data and measurable deviation. Here's how we produce and quality-check chokepoint intelligence.

Baseline Construction

Rolling baselines are computed per chokepoint using 7/30/90-day historical windows. Baselines adapt to seasonal patterns, weather cycles, and long-term trade volume trends — not static averages.

Confidence Scoring

Every alert includes a confidence score (0.0–1.0) based on data completeness, AIS message density, baseline stability, and corroborating weather/route data. Low-confidence alerts are flagged, not hidden.

False Positive Handling

Seasonal convoy scheduling, planned maintenance closures, and weather-related slowdowns are accounted for in baselines. Known recurring patterns are documented so operators can triage with context.

Known Limitations

AIS coverage depends on receiver density. Vessels with transponders disabled are invisible. Satellite AIS introduces ~15-minute latency vs. terrestrial. We document coverage gaps per chokepoint.

Data Sources

AIS position data from both terrestrial and satellite receiver networks. Weather data from maritime meteorological services. All data ingested via authenticated feeds, not web-scraped.

Refresh & Latency

Chokepoint metrics refresh every 15 minutes. End-to-end pipeline latency (AIS ingestion → baseline comparison → alert delivery) is typically under 90 seconds. Webhook delivery adds <200ms.

PROVEN IN CRISIS

Historical Disruptions

Strait-Signal models are calibrated against real-world chokepoint disruptions.

Ever Given — Suez Canal, March 2021

Six days of complete blockage. 400+ vessels queued. $9.6 billion/day in trade disrupted. Strait-Signal queue tracking and delay delta models are calibrated against this event.

Houthi Attacks — Red Sea, 2023–2024

Sustained disruption to Bab el-Mandeb transit. Major carriers rerouted via Cape of Good Hope. Route risk scoring quantifies the time and cost impact of rerouting decisions.

Panama Drought — 2023–2024

Historic low water levels cut daily transits from 36 to 24. Booking slot auctions exceeded $4 million. Drought-indexed capacity baselines now support forward-looking risk models.

Baltimore Bridge — March 2024

Port of Baltimore closed for weeks after the Key Bridge collapse. Container diversions to Norfolk, New York, and Savannah. Regional port disruption cascades tracked and scored.
