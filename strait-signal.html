COVERAGE

Seven Critical Chokepoints

Real-time monitoring of the world's most strategically important maritime corridors.

Suez Canal

The world's busiest artificial waterway. ~15% of global trade transits daily. Queue depth and northbound/southbound convoy tracking.

Bab el-Mandeb

Gateway to the Red Sea and Suez. ~10% of global seaborne oil transits. Houthi-related disruption intelligence and reroute risk scoring.

Singapore Strait

The world's busiest shipping lane. Over 1,000 vessels transit daily. Congestion monitoring and anchorage queue depth tracking.

Strait of Malacca

Connects the Indian Ocean to the Pacific. ~25% of global trade. Draught limitations and seasonal weather risk impact scoring.

Panama Canal

Connects Atlantic and Pacific. Drought-driven transit restrictions and lock queue monitoring. Historical baseline against 2023-24 drought disruptions.

Strait of Hormuz

~21% of global oil consumption transits daily. Geopolitical tension monitoring and tanker traffic flow analysis.

Port of Rotterdam

Europe's largest port and key North Sea gateway. Berth availability, container dwell time, and feeder route impact tracking.

CAPABILITIES

What Strait Signal Delivers

Anchorage Queue Tracking

Real-time vessel counts at chokepoint anchorage zones. Track queue formation and dissolution to anticipate delays before they become published.

Delay Delta Baselines

Compares current transit times against rolling historical baselines. Positive delta means delays are building. Negative means the corridor is clearing.

Threshold-Based Alerts

Configure alert thresholds per chokepoint. Get notified when queue depths, transit times, or risk scores exceed your defined tolerances.

Weather-Adjusted Risk

Factors in sea state, wind, visibility, and seasonal conditions. Risk scoring adapts to conditions that affect transit efficiency beyond vessel congestion.

Route Risk Scoring

Composite risk scores across multi-chokepoint routes. Compare Asia-Europe via Suez vs. Cape of Good Hope with quantified time and risk trade-offs.

API-First Integration

RESTful API with webhook support. Pull chokepoint status, delay metrics, and alerts into your existing logistics, TMS, or risk management stack.

PIPELINE

From Raw AIS to Actionable Intelligence

01

AIS Ingestion

Continuous ingestion of global AIS position reports from terrestrial and satellite receiver networks. Millions of messages processed daily across all seven chokepoints.

02

Baseline Computation

Rolling historical baselines are computed per chokepoint for transit times, queue depths, and vessel flow rates. Baselines adapt to seasonal patterns and long-term trends.

03

Anomaly Detection

Current conditions are compared against baselines in real time. Significant deviations trigger anomaly flags across key metrics: delay delta, queue growth rate, and weather-adjusted risk.

04

Alerts & Scoring

Breach events generate structured alerts delivered via dashboard, API, or webhook. Route risk scores aggregate chokepoint conditions into actionable corridor-level intelligence.

PROVEN IN CRISIS

Historical Disruptions

Strait Signal's models are calibrated against real-world chokepoint disruptions.

Ever Given — Suez Canal, March 2021

Six days of complete blockage. 400+ vessels queued. $9.6 billion/day in trade disrupted. Strait Signal's queue tracking and delay delta models are calibrated against this event.

Houthi Attacks — Red Sea, 2023–2024

Sustained disruption to Bab el-Mandeb transit. Major carriers rerouted via Cape of Good Hope. Route risk scoring quantifies the time and cost impact of rerouting decisions.

Panama Drought — 2023–2024

Historic low water levels cut daily transits from 36 to 24. Booking slot auctions exceeded $4 million. Drought-indexed capacity baselines now support forward-looking risk models.

Baltimore Bridge — March 2024

Port of Baltimore closed for weeks after the Key Bridge collapse. Container diversions to Norfolk, New York, and Savannah. Regional port disruption cascades tracked and scored.
